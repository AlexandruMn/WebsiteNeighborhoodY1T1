Discover Bakenes, Haarlem

Located in the heart of Haarlem, Bakenes is an area rich in history and full of fascinating places to explore. From impressive churches to lively market squares and iconic statues, Bakenes has a variety of attractions that will captivate visitors and leave lasting memories.

St. Bavo Church (Grote Kerk)

At the heart of Bakenes stands the magnificent St. Bavo Church, locally known as the Grote Kerk. This stunning Gothic cathedral is a testament to the grandeur of Dutch architecture. Its towering spires and intricate stained glass windows make it a must-visit for history buffs and admirers of architectural beauty. Inside, you'll find the impressive Müller Organ, an instrument renowned for its exquisite music.

Grote Market

The Grote Market, or Great Market, is the vibrant heart of Haarlem and a bustling hub of activity. Here you can enjoy the lively atmosphere as you stroll through the numerous market stalls, boutiques and cozy cafes. It's the perfect place to enjoy coffee, sample local sweets, and watch the world go by. Additionally, the Market Square is a hotspot for events and festivals throughout the year.

Haarlem Town Hall (Stadhuis)

Haarlem City Hall is an impressive example of Dutch Renaissance architecture. The ornate façade and magnificent interior make for a stunning sight. Visitors can take guided tours to explore the rich history and impressive art collection within the castle walls. Town Hall Square is often the setting for cultural events and festivals.

Statue of Laurens Jansz Coster

As you wander through Bakenes, don't miss the statue of Laurens Jansz Coster, a tribute to the inventor of the printing press. This bronze monument serves as a reminder of the neighborhood's historical significance in the world of printing and literature. It's an excellent spot to pause and reflect on the enduring impact of Coster's invention.
